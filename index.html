How to get an accurate reading

  1. Stand to the side of the pitching lane, roughly level with the midpoint, 15–25 ft back.
  2. Handheld? Brace your elbows against your body and hold still through the pitch — a tripod is steadier, but the app ignores camera-shake frames automatically.
  3. Frame the whole flight — pitcher near one edge, catcher near the other. The full pitching distance must be in view.
  4. Pick the age group up top so the app knows the distance (10U 35 ft · 12U 40 ft · 14U+ 43 ft).
  5. Record (the default) captures a clip you then scrub frame-by-frame for a precise number. Tap Auto ⚡ to turn on hands-free live readings — but hold the phone steady, since camera movement can trigger false readings.
  6. For the sharpest timing, record a slow-motion clip in the iPhone Camera app, then Import it here and set the exact release/catch frames.

Speed = pitching distance ÷ flight time. The reading is the average speed over the flight; turn on “Radar-style” in Settings to estimate the higher out-of-hand speed a radar gun shows.
